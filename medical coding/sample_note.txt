CHIEF COMPLAINT: Chest pain and shortness of breath.

HPI: 62-year-old male with a history of hypertension and type 2 diabetes mellitus
presents to the ED with substernal chest pain radiating to the left arm, onset
2 hours prior to arrival. Associated with diaphoresis and nausea. No prior
history of MI. Patient denies fever, cough, or recent travel.

PMH:
- Essential hypertension, controlled on lisinopril
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus without complications, on metformin
- Hyperlipidemia

ASSESSMENT:
1. Acute ST-elevation myocardial infarction of anterior wall - confirmed by
   ECG showing ST elevation in leads V1-V4 and elevated troponin.
2. Type 2 diabetes mellitus, without complications.
3. Essential hypertension, well controlled.

PLAN:
- Emergent cardiac catheterization performed; drug-eluting stent placed in
  the proximal LAD.
- Continue metformin and lisinopril.
- Start dual antiplatelet therapy.
